## Business Type

Checkpoint64 is a gaming utility software company that provides automatic cloud
backup, full version history, one-click restore, and co-op save sharing for PC
game save files. The product targets a high-friction, high-emotion problem for
gamers: corrupted saves, overwritten co-op worlds, host-dependent worlds nobody
else can access, and the ongoing chaos of manually managing save folders across
devices and players. Its core offering is a desktop app for Windows, macOS
(Apple Silicon), and Linux that watches game save folders, checks for changes
every 30 seconds, uploads only the files that changed, keeps every version, and
lets users restore any prior save state in seconds. The company differentiates
through a gamer-native product experience: presets for 60+ games plus 7
emulators, server-enforced lock-based co-op world handoff (with expiring locks
and a shared logbook), and read-only creator share codes. Its business model
pairs a genuinely free entry plan with a one-time "Lifetime" upgrade for more
space, plus a "Pro" tier for crews, streamers, and modding groups who need more
seats, storage, and community share codes — available as a one-time purchase on
Steam, so even the top tier avoids a subscription. Nothing expires and nothing
recurs, positioning the brand as a practical alternative to both manual backup
workflows and expensive, underused dedicated servers.

## Brand Voice & Tone

The brand voice is irreverent, confident, and gamer-authentic. It speaks like an
experienced player who understands the pain of lost progress, co-op friction,
and modded save instability, and it expresses that understanding through sharp
humor, concise copy, and anti-corporate positioning such as "free plan, actually
free," "pay once for more space," and "no 'powered by AI.'" It leans on concrete,
physical metaphors — saves as cartridges, a game library as a cartridge shelf —
and honest, self-deprecating founder energy ("built by people who lost a
200-hour Factorio base and never got over it"). The result is a blend of
technical competence and community fluency that makes the product feel
trustworthy without sounding sterile or enterprise-heavy.

The "no subscription, ever" line holds and is on-brand: Free and Lifetime never
expire, and even Pro can be bought as a one-time purchase on Steam. Lead with the
money story the audience actually wants — "free plan, actually free," "pay once,
keep it forever," "no 'powered by AI.'"

## Target Audience

Checkpoint64 targets PC gamers in the US and other English-speaking markets who
actively invest time in save-dependent games such as Minecraft, Stardew Valley,
Factorio, Valheim, Satisfactory, Elden Ring, Palworld, and modded titles — plus
retro and emulator players who want real version history for their save states.
The audience spans solo players, co-op groups, modding communities, speedrun
teams, and content creators, generally ranging from teens through adults in their
20s, 30s, and 40s who are comfortable installing desktop software and managing
game libraries. Psychographically, these users value control, permanence,
convenience, and protection against avoidable disasters; they are frustrated by
save corruption, accidental overwrites, host dependency, and expensive dedicated
server costs for casual play schedules. They range from moderately technical to
highly technical, but they do not want to babysit file systems or build DIY
backup workflows. They choose Checkpoint64 because it turns save management into
an automatic, low-maintenance system while also enabling collaborative play,
creator distribution, and cost savings over recurring server rentals.

## Key Themes

- Automatic cloud backup for game saves — checks every 30 seconds, hands-off
- Full version history with instant one-click restore
- Co-op world sharing with server-enforced, lock-based control (expiring locks + handoff warnings)
- Save corruption recovery for modded and vanilla games
- Incremental, deduplicated uploads — only changed files are sent
- Shared activity logbook — who uploaded, restored, or grabbed the lock, and when
- Read-only creator share codes for fan-accessible save files (Pro)
- Dedicated-server cost savings for casual co-op groups
- Cross-platform save protection for Windows, macOS (Apple Silicon), and Linux
- Presets for 60+ games and 7 emulators
- No subscription, ever — free plan, one-time Lifetime upgrade, and Pro as a one-time purchase on Steam
