▮ FEATURES

WHAT'S IN THE BOX.

Built by people who reload saves a lot. No fluff, no charge per person, no “powered by AI.” Just a save vault that works.

▮ VERSION HISTORY

EVERY UPLOAD IS A VERSION.

Scroll through every backup with file count, size, and what changed since last time. Hit Restore and the files go back on disk, marked as current — no guessing, no “final_v2_REAL” folders.

▮ CO-OP LOCKS

ONE PERSON HOLDS THE WORLD.

Games like Factorio, Valheim, and Satisfactory have one live world at a time. Whoever holds the lock uploads; everyone else downloads. Holder gone quiet? Locks expire on their own, and you can take over — with a warning, and a logbook entry so everyone knows.

▮ ONLY WHAT CHANGED

TINY UPLOADS.

Only the files that changed get uploaded — renamed files cost nothing extra. A 500 MB Minecraft world re-uploads as a few MB after a normal session, not the whole thing. Easy on your internet, easy on your storage.

▮ 60+ GAMES READY

SET UP IN SECONDS.

Presets for 60+ games — four flavours of modded Minecraft, Stardew, Skyrim, Palworld, Elden Ring — plus seven emulators. Pick which files count and skip the screenshots. If it writes saves to a folder, it works.

▮ SHARE CODES

ONE WORLD, WHOLE CROWD.

Running a community world? Mint a join code and anyone holding it can download your save — but never upload over it. Codes are capped and revocable, and read-only visitors don’t use up seats. (Pro)

▮ LOGBOOK

WHO DID WHAT, WHEN.

▮ FAQ

FREQUENTLY CHECKED.

WHAT COUNTS AS A 'SAVE'?
Whatever your game writes to your hard drive. Checkpoint64 treats the files in a folder as one save and backs them up together. Presets for 60+ games (and seven emulators) set this up for you; for anything else, point at the folder and pick the files yourself.
DO YOU UPLOAD WHILE THE GAME IS RUNNING?
Yes, carefully. Checkpoint64 never locks your save files. It checks the folder every 30 seconds; if the game is mid-save it waits for things to go quiet and tries again — no broken files, no stutter. The app naps between checks, so you won’t notice it while playing.
WHAT IF MY CO-OP PARTNER OVERWRITES MY UPLOAD?
They can’t, on purpose. Only the person holding the lock can upload. To push their version they have to take the lock first — which warns you, and goes in the logbook for all to see. Worst case, your version is one Restore away in the history.
DO I STILL NEED A DEDICATED SERVER?
For most groups, no. The whole point of a dedicated server is keeping your world online when the host’s PC is off. Checkpoint64 covers about 90% of that for a one-time fee: whoever wants to play grabs the lock, plays their session, then pushes the save back. A typical co-op group saves €120–€240 a year compared to renting a 24/7 server that sits idle 18 hours a day.
DOES THIS WORK FOR EMULATORS OR CONSOLE SAVES?
Emulators, absolutely — RetroArch, Dolphin, PCSX2, DuckStation, PPSSPP, RPCS3, and Cemu all have presets, so your save states finally get real version history. Console saves only work if you can get them onto a PC first. The app itself runs on Windows, macOS (Apple Silicon), and Linux.
WHAT DOES IT COST?
The free plan is real and stays free: 20 MiB, your own space plus one team. Lifetime is a one-time payment — 2.5 GiB per space, up to 3 teams, bought direct or through Steam. Pro is for big crews: 50 GiB per space, 5 teams, 25 seats each, read-only share codes. No charge per person on any tier. Final numbers land with v1 — join the list and lock in the early-bird price.
CAN I USE IT TODAY?
Yes — the early build is free to download right now for Windows, macOS (Apple Silicon), and Linux. v1 and the Steam release are coming later this year; the list gets exactly one email the day they land.
WHO CAN SEE MY SAVES?
Your teammates — and only the ones you invite. They see your display name, never your email. And your data stays yours: export everything as a zip whenever you like, and deleting your account actually deletes it (after a 7-day cooling-off period, in case of 2am regret).
